Setup & User Guide

Welcome to Willow

Willow is your personal AI practice assistant. She lives on your phone, knows your clients, and handles the day-to-day tasks of running your practice — so you can focus on the work that matters.

After a simple one-time setup, Willow can do everything from your phone: manage clients, generate documents, send emails, schedule sessions, track payments, and more. Just tell her what you need, or tap a button. She handles the rest.

What Willow Can Do

  • Manage your clients — browse, create, and update client records
  • Generate documents — session notes, integration guides, summaries, and more
  • Send emails — onboarding, templates, newsletters, and offers
  • Schedule sessions — add and manage Google Calendar events
  • Track payments — record payments and send receipts
  • Run your Soul Emergence program — send weekly workbooks and manage journals
  • Answer questions — ask anything about your practice data
  • Remember things — save notes, reminders, and client observations
How it works: Willow connects to your existing Google Sheets practice system. All your data stays in your Google account — nothing is stored externally. She simply reads and writes to your spreadsheet, exactly like the existing sidebar, but through a warm, conversational interface you can use from anywhere.

Before You Begin

Setting up Willow takes about 30 minutes. Here's what you'll need before we start.

What You'll Need

1
A Google Account
Willow runs entirely within Google's ecosystem — Sheets, Drive, Gmail, and Calendar. You'll need the Google account that holds your practice spreadsheet.
2
Your Practice Spreadsheet
The Google Sheet with your client data, templates, and practice records. If you're starting fresh, Willow's setup will create the structure for you.
3
An Anthropic API Key
Willow uses Claude AI to understand your requests and take action. You'll need an API key from console.anthropic.com. If you received a trial key, you can use that to get started and add your own later.
4
30 Minutes of Quiet Time
The setup is straightforward, but give yourself space to move through each step without rushing. Once it's done, you won't need to do it again.
About Your API Key

Your API key is what connects Willow to the AI that powers her. Think of it as a private passcode between your Willow and the AI service. It's stored securely in your Google project and never shared with anyone.

Trial key: If you were given a trial key, it will work for your first week. After that, you can either get your own key at console.anthropic.com or contact your provider about a monthly plan.

One-Time Setup

Follow these steps once. After this, Willow is yours — always ready on your phone.

Step 1: Open Apps Script

1
Open your practice spreadsheet
Go to your Google Sheet in a web browser (this works best on a computer).
2
Open the script editor
Click Extensions in the menu bar, then Apps Script. This opens the code editor in a new tab.

Step 2: Add the Code Files

In the Apps Script editor, you'll create three new files. For each one:

3
Create the API file
Click the + next to "Files" → select Script → name it EmeraldAPI. Paste the provided API code into this file.
4
Create the AI file
Click + → Script → name it EmeraldAI. Paste the provided AI code.
5
Create the UI file
Click + → HTML → name it EmeraldUI. Paste the provided interface code.
6
Save everything
Press Ctrl+S (or Cmd+S on Mac) to save all files. You should see three new files in the left sidebar alongside the existing code.
Don't worry — adding these files won't affect anything that's already working. Your existing sidebar, forms, and automations continue to run exactly as before.
3

Step 3: Run First-Time Setup

7
Go back to your spreadsheet
Switch back to the Google Sheet tab. Refresh the page so the new menu items appear.
8
Run the setup
In the menu bar, click Doula Tools → Setup → Configure Settings. This creates your default configuration. When prompted, enter your practice name, your name, and your preferred AI assistant name.

Step 4: Enter Your API Key

9
Open Project Settings
In the Apps Script editor, click the gear icon (Project Settings) in the left sidebar. Scroll down to Script Properties.
10
Add your API key
Click Add Script Property. Set the name to ANTHROPIC_API_KEY and paste your API key as the value. Click Save.

Step 5: Deploy

11
Create the Web App
In the Apps Script editor, click Deploy → New deployment. For type, select Web app. Set "Execute as" to Me and "Who has access" to Only myself. Click Deploy.
12
Authorize permissions
Google will ask you to authorize. Click Authorize access, choose your account, and if you see "Google hasn't verified this app," click Advanced → Go to [project name]. This is safe — it's your own code running in your own account.
13
Copy your Willow URL
After deploying, you'll see a Web App URL. Copy this link — this is your Willow's permanent home address.

Step 6: Add to Your Phone

14
Open the URL on your phone
Send yourself the Web App URL (text, email, or notes app) and open it in Safari or Chrome on your phone.
15
Add to Home Screen
iPhone: Tap the share button (square with arrow) → Add to Home Screen.
Android: Tap the three-dot menu → Add to Home Screen.

Willow now appears as an app icon on your phone. Tap it anytime to open.
That's it. You're done.

Willow is now ready. Open her from your home screen and start managing your practice with a simple conversation.

Memory & Reminders

Willow remembers context within your session and can save important notes for the long term.

Session Memory

Within a single session (up to 24 hours of activity), Willow remembers your conversation history and recent actions. She knows which client you're working with and what you've already done. This means you can have natural, flowing conversations without repeating yourself.

Long-Term Memory

Ask Willow to remember something and she'll save it permanently:

"Remember that Sarah prefers morning appointments"

"Note: Emma is traveling in April, reschedule her Week 4"

"Remind me to follow up with Kim about her journal"

These notes persist across sessions. Client-specific notes appear automatically when you select that client. Pinned reminders appear at the start of every session.

Managing Templates

Templates are the Google Docs that Willow copies and fills with client data when generating documents. You manage them through the Template Registry.

The Template Registry

Your spreadsheet has a special sheet called Template Registry that lists every template type and its connected Google Doc ID. This is how Willow knows which template to use for each document.

Wiring a Template

You can connect templates two ways:

Via the Menu

1
Go to Doula Tools → Manage Templates
2
Search for your template by name — it searches your Google Drive
3
Click Wire next to the correct document to connect it

Via Chat

No redeployment needed. Changing settings or template IDs takes effect immediately — just refresh Willow in your browser.

Privacy & Security

Your client data is precious. Here's exactly how Willow handles it.

Where Your Data Lives

All your data stays within Google's infrastructure — Google Sheets, Google Drive, Gmail, and Google Calendar. Willow does not store client data anywhere outside your Google account.

What the AI Sees

When you ask Willow a question or trigger an action, she sends your conversation to the Claude AI service for processing. What gets sent:

  • Your message and recent conversation history
  • The currently selected client's basic info (name, status, type)
  • Any specific cell data needed for the action

The AI does not receive your entire spreadsheet. Only the minimal data needed for your current request is included.

Who Has Access

Your Willow Web App runs as your Google account with access set to "Only myself." No one else can open your Willow URL unless they are signed in to your Google account.

Your API Key

Your Anthropic API key is stored in Google Apps Script's Script Properties — a secure, server-side storage mechanism. It is never exposed in the browser, never included in client-facing code, and never shared with anyone.
